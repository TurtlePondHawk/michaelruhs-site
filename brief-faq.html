Structural Risk Brief — FAQ

What exactly is the Structural Risk Brief?

A document-based structural exposure assessment of referral concentration and Medicare Advantage dependency. It models sensitivity under modest stress assumptions and identifies stabilization priorities.

Is this a consulting engagement?

No. The Brief is a contained structural analysis. It does not include advisory services or operational redesign.

Why is there no introductory call?

The Brief is designed to be document-driven. Submitted inputs are sufficient to model structural exposure. Clarifications are handled via email if required.

Why is payment required upfront?

The Brief involves allocated modeling time. Work begins upon receipt of payment and is non-refundable once analysis starts.

How long is the Brief?

Typically 6–10 pages depending on structural complexity.

What inputs are required?

• Five Snapshot section scores
• Top 2 referral percentages
• Top 3 referral percentages
• Largest Medicare Advantage percentage
• Approximate annual revenue
• One sentence describing your primary structural concern

Is this confidential?

Yes. Submitted information is treated confidentially and used solely for modeling.
