Structural Risk Brief — FAQ

Clarification of scope, process, and positioning.

What exactly is the Structural Risk Brief?

A document-based structural exposure assessment of referral concentration and Medicare Advantage dependency within your revenue model. It evaluates sensitivity under modest stress assumptions and identifies stabilization priorities.

Is this a strategy or consulting engagement?

No. The Brief is a contained structural analysis. It does not redesign operations, negotiate contracts, or provide ongoing advisory services.

Why is there no introductory call?

The Brief is designed to be document-driven. The required submission data is sufficient to model structural exposure. Clarifications, if needed, are handled via email to preserve analytical efficiency.

Why is payment required upfront?

The Brief involves allocated modeling time. Work begins upon receipt of payment and is non-refundable once analysis starts.

How long is the Brief?

Typically 6–10 pages depending on structural complexity. It is concise and focused rather than expansive.

What inputs are required?

• Five Snapshot section scores
• Top 2 referral percentages
• Top 3 referral percentages
• Largest Medicare Advantage percentage
• Approximate annual revenue
• One sentence describing your primary structural concern

How is this different from what a CPA or internal analyst would review?

CPAs evaluate historical performance. Internal analysts often assess volume and margin trends. The Structural Risk Brief models dependency sensitivity and leverage exposure under structural change.

Is this confidential?

Yes. Submitted information is treated confidentially and used solely for the modeling exercise.
